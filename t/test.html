WHO note

WHO comment


This substance is an antibody drug conjugate (ADC) composed by a monoclonal IgG1 antibody targeting TNFRSF17 (BCMA, CD269) conjugated covalently to the dibenzocyclooctyne noncleavable linker-maytansinoid warhead.

It is produced using a proprietary cell free protein synthesis technology called XpressCF+® from Sutro Biopharma and it is non-glycosylated.

The INN request for the naked antibody was not submitted, however it was already requested to the applicant.

This substance is under development for the treatment Multiple myeloma. The information retrieved from Integrity is enclosed.

To date there are six INN for mAbs targeting TNFRSF17: teclistamab (120)(82), pacanalotamab (123)(85), pavurutamab (123)(85), and alnuctamab (123)(85), which are bispecifics with CD3E, all in Phase I for Multiple myeloma; and belantamab (118)(80) and its conjugate belantamab mafodotin (118)(80) (the ADC form is registered for multiple myeloma).

Five two-word names have been suggested with the mAb containing the infix -ta- and the payload suffix -tansine. The INN Secretariat requests the chemists to confirm if indeed the conjugate is a -tansine.

Regarding the mAb part:

The fourth one, spefitamab, may be in conflict with spesolimab (119)(81), glofitamab (121)(83) and upifitamab rilsodotin (123)(85).
The fifth one, adcinctamab, may be in conflict with idactamab (123)(85), fidasimtamab (RN 11670) and Adcin® (adapalene (64)(32), clindamycin (21)(9)).

The second one, dacnextamab, may be available, the conflict with odronextamab (121)(83) and Dacnel® capsules (minocycline (14)(6)) seems to be small.

The first, decobatamab, and third, ispectamab, suggested names seem to be available.

Regarding the payload:

dezotansine may be in conflict with nendratareotide uzatansine (124), naratuximab emtansine (114)(76) and dezocine (35)(16).
dibotansine seems to be available.

decobatamab dibotansine is proposed. Please comment.
